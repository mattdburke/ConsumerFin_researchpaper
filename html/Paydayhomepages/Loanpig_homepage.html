What Is The Difference Between A Payday Loan & A Bank Loan?

One of the biggest differences between a payday loan and a bank loan is that the bank loan is often a larger amount over a longer period of time. Payday loans however are designed to be paid back over a short period of time. In addition to this, some bank loans can be secured loans, rather than an unsecured status of a payday loan. This means that your loan amount may be held against an asset that you possess, such as your home or your car. If you miss a repayment on a secured loan, your assets may be at risk of repossession in order for the bank or financial institute to get their money back. With LoanPig however, our loans are totally unsecured, and while missing repayment can have serious financial consequences, your assets are not at risk.

What Is APR?

When dealing with any form of financial lending, whether that's from a bank, a payday loan lender or another alternative, one of the most common things that you will see is APR. APR stands for annual percentage rate and this provides you with the information you need to determine how much your loan will cost you in total. APR is calculated over the length of a year, and includes both interest rate and any fees or charges the lender is placing on the loan.

How Can APR Affect My Short Term Loan?

APR can increase your loan amount and can tell you how much your loan will cost you to pay back. The Financial Conduct Authority has recently placed various caps on aspects of APR like interest rate and charges, so customers no longer have to pay back more than double what they have borrowed. Interest rates have been capped at 0.8% per day, and charges such as a default fee have been capped at £15. This means that customers are better protected than ever when it comes to borrowing a short term loan with the likes of LoanPig and other lenders.

What Happens If I Miss A Repayment On My Short Term Loan?

Missing repayments can have a serious financial implication, but some lenders can provide you with help if you need it. Some lenders will offer things like rollovers, renewals or a flexible payment plan if you make them aware that you may miss a repayment before it happens. If you do miss a repayment, you may be subject to a default fee, and interest rates will continue to accrue until the loan is paid.
