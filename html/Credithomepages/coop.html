Balance transfer

A balance transfer is when you move an unpaid balance from one credit card to another, typically in order to pay it off at a lower interest rate. When you do a balance transfer, you effectively use one credit card to fully or partially pay off the other. We may charge you a fee for doing a balance transfer.

Credit limit

Introductory offers

If you’re taking out a new product with The Co-operative Bank, you may benefit from an introductory offer, such as 0% interest, for a limited time. It’s important when applying for a credit card that you understand the terms and conditions of introductory offers.

×

Annual fees

Some lenders charge annual fees for using their products. These are typically charged automatically once a year and are added to your balance.

×

Cashback on purchases

Some credit cards pay cashback on purchases, effectively earning you a discount. Cashback typically comes to you as a credit to your balance, paid monthly or annually. Cashback on purchases is not the same as the cashback you may get on your debit card when making a purchase.

×

Cash advance fees

If you use your credit card to withdraw money from an ATM, we’ll charge a cash advance fee. You’ll also be charged interest on the amount you withdraw. It’s typically cheaper to use your debit card to withdraw cash.

×

Contactless

Contactless is a secure, fast and convenient way to make card purchases. Contactless payments are limited to £30 per transaction. Instead of entering a PIN, you touch your card against the card reader. Contactless technology uses the same security as chip and PIN, so you’re protected from fraud.

×

Chip and PIN

Chip and PIN is proven to reduce fraud by making card transactions even more secure. When making a card payment, just enter the same PIN you use when using an ATM. Never write down your PIN or share it with anyone else.

×

Additional cardholders

Adding an additonal cardholder to your credit card account can be a convenient way to manage shared finances. You each have a card and share the credit limit. However, unlike with joint current accounts where both account holders are liable, the primary credit cardholder is solely responsible for the debt.

×

Eligibility

Our eligibility criteria helps us make responsible lending decisions. It’s good for customers to know our eligibility criteria before applying. Eligibility is not a guarantee of acceptance.

×

Interest free purchases

Some credit cards allow you to make purchases without paying interest, typically for a limited time and subject to you making your payments on time. This can be a good way to spread the payment of larger purchases. Interest free purchases don’t include cash withdrawals and balance transfers.
